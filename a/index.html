№ 01 / on tending

Every family deserves to be tended to.

Tending is a small word for a big job. Before the birth, it looks like questions and answers: the long ones, the embarrassing ones, the ones you didn't know to ask. During the birth, it looks like a hand on a shoulder, water at the right temperature, a steady voice when the room gets loud.

The work is not glamorous and it is not optional. It is what families have done for each other for as long as there have been families.

Birth designed for you and yours.
№ 02 / what you can be tended in

Holding space for all kinds of birth.

  • I Your village Before, during, and after your birth: the people, the rhythm, the room.
  • II Your vision What you want this birth to feel like, before anyone else gets a vote.
  • III Your choices There are more options than the pamphlet shows. We'll go through them.
  • IV Your partner So they can show up ready and supported, not stranded in the corner.
  • V Your questions Yes, all of them. The first consult is free.
  • VI Your care team The circle around your birth: providers, family, friends, on your terms.
№ 03 / about

A short note from Melissa.

I'm Melissa Cunningham. I trained as a birth doula and I work in Los Angeles and the surrounding county. I came to this work the way most doulas do, by being in a room where someone needed it and realizing the room needed more.

I work with first-time parents, second-time parents, partners, queer families, single parents by choice, and anyone whose birth doesn't fit a brochure. I don't have a script. We figure out what your kind of birth looks like, and then I help you get there.
